New in 3.0.
If your connection drops while you are in a room, a game, or watching one, the server now holds your place for 45 seconds and the game keeps trying to reconnect, so getting back in time drops you right back where you were, in the same room or game with your wood, your score, and your host duties intact, instead of sending you out to the lobby. If your game or room ends while you are away you return to wherever you would have been sent, your room or the lobby, and if you do not make it back in time, or the server itself restarts, you come back to the lobby as before.
There is now a team chat channel, opened with the semicolon key while you are in a game, for talking to your own team, with its own team chats buffer and support for the me action and slash commands just like the other channels. For now everyone in a game counts as one team, so it reaches the whole game, but once the player versus player mode arrives it will reach only your side; staff can mute a player's team chat like any other channel.
In an environment versus player game, the builder bots and the cave's wood now sound both muffled and reverberant from outside the walls, as if you are hearing the inside of the cave leak out to you through the stone, while your own sounds on the exterior stay clear. The muffling is a low pass filter that cuts the brightness so the sound feels like it is behind a wall, and it is a first taste of the through the wall audio the future player versus player mode will use on both sides.
Placing wood to reinforce a wall now plays its sound in 3D at that wall, so you hear which side you are building on, off to your left when you are at the left wall for example. Everyone else in the game hears each placement at the wall too, so they can tell exactly where the reinforcing is happening.
The inside of the cave now carries a natural reverb, so footsteps, wall hits, wood, and the other sounds down in the cavern reverberate the way they would in a real enclosed cave, with a soft tail of reflections rather than distinct echoes; the open-air outdoor areas and the outdoor watcher deck stay dry, with no reverb.
Your starting wood and your best round bonus wood now arrive as a single random size rather than a mix, so one game you might open with all small pieces and another with all large. The game over reward now names the size you earned, for example thirty two large starter wood, so you know exactly what is waiting for you next round.
In an environment versus player game, the builder bots now follow the same rule when they repair a wall, so a bot that carries a larger piece of wood mends more than one hauling a small piece.
The size of the wood you place now matters when you reinforce a wall: larger pieces add more strength, with small giving five to ten, medium ten to twenty, and large fifteen to thirty, echoing the tiers of the enemies' weapons. Reinforcing always spends your biggest piece first, so seeking out the large wood with the D key is now worth the trip.
A player's information card, opened from the F6 players menu, now shows whether that player has agreed to the server rules, so you can tell at a glance who is able to join a room or game with you.
When you first sign in you must now read the server rules and agree to them before you can play: type /rules to read the player rules, then /rules agree to enter the lobby, or /rules disagree to leave. Everyone must read the player rules to agree, staff must read both the player and staff rules, and a player may open the staff rules only after reading their own. You only agree once, and are asked again only if the server's rules are updated.
Staff can now review a player's warning history with the new /warnings command, which opens a read-only list of every warning that player has received, each showing its level, who gave it, and the reason.
Wood now drops in three sizes, small, medium, and large, each with its own sounds and chosen at random as it falls. Pressing D to find nearby wood now tells you each piece's size, and pressing I to check your wood now breaks your total down by size, such as fourteen small, five medium, and twenty-two large pieces.
Reviewing your message buffers now stops firmly at the ends: moving past the first or last buffer, or the first or last message within one, no longer does anything, so you can feel the edges. The exception is a buffer or message list with only one entry, which still reads out each time you press, so it is never left silent.
In a player versus environment game, the enemies now add to their numbers every second round instead of every round, so the difficulty climbs more gently and a run lasts longer.
The main menu now has a documentation menu, listed abuv the connection menu, where you can read the changelog, credits, readme, and todo list without connecting to a server.
You can now read the server's rules in game with the /rules command: type /rules for the player rules, or /rules player or /rules staff to read either page. Anyone can read both, including the rules staff are held to, so you can always see the standards staff must follow.
For server hosts, the help pages have moved into a docks/help subfolder, named player.txt and staff.txt, with a new docks/rules subfolder reserved for later; players see no change beyond a cleaner help window title.
The readme now includes a full keyboard reference that lists every key the game uses, grouped by purpose.
The game now comes with a readme that explains how everything works, moving around, chatting, the players menu, rooms, and both game modes. The in-game help, opened with /help, is now just the list of chat commands.
Updated the todo list file with the latest bug fix and feature improvements made to the game.
New in 2.9.
The F6 players menu now shows each player's current language channel beside their name, so you can tell who is on which channel at a glance.
Staff now have action buttons on a player's information card, opened from the F6 players menu: kick, ban, promote, demote, notify, and warn, each shown only when your rank allows it on that player. Choosing one walks you through any details it needs, such as a ban length, a new rank, a notification, or a warning level and reason, then carries out the same action as the matching command, with the same rules.
The player information card, opened by choosing a player in the F6 players menu, now has a Mute this player checkbox and a Private message this player button. Muting someone hides their global chat, local chat, private messages, and voice from you only, and it stays muted across sessions until you uncheck it; the player is never told.
In an environment versus player game, the round timer now counts down only while you can actually attack, so the rest windows where your swings ricochet no longer eat into your time. Rounds also begin with more time than before, and each round you clear adds ten more seconds, so a game no longer ends as quickly.
The L key now works inside a game too, reading out who is in the lobby, the same as it already does from a room or the lobby itself.
The voice key now behaves the same whether or not hold to talk is on: both ways are limited to one start or stop every half second, so quickly tapping the key can no longer spam the voice on and off sounds.
Voice chat no longer drives your ping up or drops the connection when two or more people talk at once. Voice is now sent in a way that simply skips the occasional lost moment of audio instead of forcing the whole connection to resend it, so talking stays smooth even over a slower link.
You can now press F6 to open a players menu that lists everyone on the server, each shown with their away status, rank, and name. Choose a player to open a read-only information card about them, with their username, nickname, pronouns, channel, away state, location, and game version; staff also see the player's mute, ban, and warning details.
Choosing which saved server or account to use has moved into the Server storage and Account storage menus, where it is now a Select option, instead of being a separate item in the connection menu.
The F2 and F4 keys have swapped: F2 now reads the server's message of the day, and F4 now reports the server's uptime.
New in 2.8.
When you create a new account, it now also asks whether to add it to your account storage, before asking whether to log in.
The sign in option now shows which account it will use, for example Sign in as Bob, and only appears once you have set up or selected an account.
The sign in as option has been replaced by Set up an existing account, which remembers an account so you can sign in as it and offers to save it to your account storage, without signing you in right away.
You can now save several accounts on your computer: the connection menu has a new Account storage option to add, edit, and remove them, and a Select an account option to choose which saved account you are signed in as.
Saved servers are now managed from a new Server storage option in the connection menu, where you add, edit, and remove them; the preferences general tab now just holds reset and save settings.
The channel menu now shows how many players are on each language channel, for example English (English) 26 players in this channel, so you can see which channels are active before you switch.
Private room passwords are now stored encrypted on the server instead of in plain text; because of this, every existing private room password must be set again from the room menu.
The kick menu in a room now shows each player by their nickname, the same as the kick menu in a game, rather than always showing their account name.
Staff are now told whenever another staff member promotes, demotes, unbans, or sends a private notification to a player, so these actions leave a record the whole team can see.
When a staff member changes the message of the day, everyone is now told in their alerts who changed it and what it was changed to, instead of it changing silently.
New in 2.7.
You can now kick yourself from your own room or game; both the Kick from room and Kick from game lists now include your own name, which you can pick to simply leave, just like the normal leave options do.
In an environment versus player game, each round now alternates between attack windows, when your swings tear the walls down, and rest windows, when your swings only ricochet while the builder bots gather wood and repair the damage; part of every hit you land is permanent, so the bots can never fully rebuild a wall you have been working on and you always gain ground.
In an environment versus player game, a round can no longer end early for lack of progress; the only way to lose is to run out the wave's time.
Fixed a bug in an environment versus player game where right after you chose to play again, your weapon could strike a wall from well out of range until you moved.
Account passwords are now protected with much stronger, modern encryption; because of this, every existing account must be created again, as older accounts can no longer sign in.
Usernames and passwords can no longer contain spaces, and the username, password, and private room password fields now reject the space key as you type.
When your game version does not match the server's, you are now clearly told that your client is incompatible and asked to update, rather than the connection simply failing.
Staff commands that target a player now accept the name typed in any capitalization, so a command works whether or not it matches the account's exact case.
You can no longer set your nickname to another account's username, so no one can borrow another player's identity while they are signed out.
The server now firmly ties each player to the account they signed in with, closing a loophole that could let a modified client appear as someone else.
New in 2.6.
In an EVP game, press B to hear where the builder bots are, by direction and distance, nearest first, the same way the P key locates other players.
You can now play a brand new game mode, EVP, short for environment versus player, the mirror of the original: instead of defending the four walls you attack them from outside with weapons drawn on the number row, while builder bots roam the cavern gathering the wood that drops to repair them. Each round opens with a build phase where your swings only ricochet, then a wave with a time limit to bring all four walls down, and every round you clear sends more bots to defend the next. The game ends when the bots hold you off, by running out the clock or stalling you out, and you are scored by the rounds you cleared.
Enemy weapon damage is random again instead of a fixed amount, and the ranges are wider than before: the axe does one to three, the bat two to four, the crowbar three to six, and the hammer four to eight, so heavier weapons can land a real blow against a full strength wall instead of barely chipping it.
Starting a game now begins by choosing a game mode, currently just PVE, before you pick who can play and the map size, making room for more modes to come.
Kicking a player from a game now returns them to wherever they came from: a player who joined through your room goes back to the room as before, but one you invited in from the lobby is sent back to the lobby instead.
When you invite a player into your game, you now first choose whether to invite someone from your room or straight from the lobby, so you can pull a lobby player right into the cavern, skipping the room and its password. A player you invited from the lobby is sent back to the lobby when they leave the game or it ends, rather than being left in your room.
Local chat now reaches everyone sharing your space, whatever language channel they are on, so people in the same room or the lobby no longer have to switch to the same channel just to talk. Global chat is still split by language channel.
You can now set the pronouns used when messages refer to you, with the new slash pronoun command: masculine, feminine, singular they, it, or none, which simply uses your name instead. Creating an account no longer asks for a gender; your pronouns default to singular they, can be changed any time, and are saved for next time.
Pressing R while in a game now reads out who is waiting in the game's room, rather than only who is in the game with you, so you can see at a glance who you could invite in.
You can now invite and kick players in your games, not just your rooms. If you host an open game, the round menu gains an invite to game option that pulls someone from your room into the game, which they answer with /accept or /reject just like a room invite, and a kick from game option that removes a player back to the room. The room menu's own options are now named invite to room and kick from room so it is always clear which space you are acting on.
New in 2.5.
You can no longer watch your own game; a game you host or are playing in no longer shows up in the watch list, since there would be nothing for you to see.
You can now watch a game instead of playing it. Choose watch game from the room menu to pick any game in the room, including single-player games and ones already underway, and up to four people can watch a game at once. You float on a deck above the cavern and roam it freely while the whole battle plays out below you, fenced in by an indestructible border, and the players are told you are watching. You take no part, so you carry no wood, cannot reinforce, and the enemies ignore you, but you hear everything and can still chat and use voice with the players; press escape to stop watching and return to the room.
You can now invite people to your room. Anyone in a room can choose invite a player from the room menu to see who is in the lobby and pick someone; that player is told privately and joins by typing /accept, or refuses with /reject, with an invitation lasting two minutes. Accepting takes them straight in, even past a private room's password, and you are told whether they accepted or declined. If an invitation runs out before it is answered, the invited player is told that it expired.
The game over screen now lays out your results in full, listing the wood you gathered, the rounds you completed, and how long you survived, and whenever you beat your best round ever you earn a bonus of twenty to forty wood that is waiting in your stockpile when the next round begins; the ordinary starting wood has been lowered from fifteen to thirty down to ten to twenty to make room for it.
You can now adjust the ambience and music volumes while you play: page up and page down change the music, and home and end change the ambience, with each setting remembered for next time.
Each piece of wood you spend reinforcing a wall now adds ten to twenty strength instead of five to ten, so repairs go further.
The limit on attackers has been removed, so every round now adds one more bot to each wall with no upper bound instead of leveling off.
Enemy damage is now a fixed amount per weapon instead of a random range, with the axe doing one, the bat two, the crowbar three, and the hammer four, so a hit always takes off a known, predictable amount.
Pressing tab during a rest now tells you which round you just finished, for example round three complete.

New in 2.4.
You can now choose which recording device voice chat uses. In preferences, under the sound tab, the new select recording device option lists your microphones; pick one or leave it on Default, and your choice is saved for next time.
The voice chat system has been completely recoded. Your voice now streams live while you hold the talk key, so others hear you in real time as you speak instead of waiting for a recorded clip to finish and send, and it still plays from your spot on the map and follows you as you move. The option to hear your own voice now applies to this live stream, is renamed to hearing your voice when streaming, and is off by default.
The enemies now hit a little softer, especially the heavier weapons. Each weapon's damage has been lowered and made more consistent, with the crowbar and hammer in particular no longer landing the occasional huge blow, so a cluster of bots can no longer strip a well-built wall as quickly and surviving comes down to steady upkeep rather than bad luck.
You can now save your servers as presets instead of retyping an address every time. In preferences, under the general tab, you can add a server by giving it a name, an address, and a port, edit any of those details on a saved one, or remove one you no longer need, and your list is kept for next time. The connection menu lets you load a saved server to connect to, showing which one is currently set, and also lets you change the server address or port directly without saving a preset, with every change remembered the moment you make it. The load list opens on a Custom entry, which simply keeps whatever address you have typed in, so a non-preset server now reads as Custom rather than none.
You now get a heads-up before every wave: about ten seconds before the enemies attack, whether it is the very first wave during the build phase or a later one during a rest, everyone in the game hears a warning sound and an alert telling you how long until they come, so a wave never catches you off guard.
You can now toggle auto-running with Alt and R wherever you can move: when it is on you run by default and hold a movement key modifier to walk, and when it is off it is the other way around, so you no longer have to hold a key down to keep up a run; the game says auto running enabled or disabled as you toggle it, and your choice is remembered for next time.
Each enemy wave is now announced with its round number, for example Round 1, the enemies are coming, and the count climbs with every fresh attack, so you can tell at a glance how deep into a round you are; pressing tab for the round status reads the round number too.
In a game, the host now opens the round menu with enter instead of tab, and anyone can press tab to hear the current round status, such as how long is left in the build phase or whether the enemies are attacking or resting.
The end-of-round message now reads your survival time in minutes and seconds instead of a raw seconds count, for example one minute and thirty-two seconds rather than ninety-two seconds.
You can no longer reinforce a wall while the enemies are actively attacking. Reinforcing now works only during the build phase and the rest periods between attacks, so shoring up a wall is a between-wave decision rather than something you can do in the middle of an assault.
New in 2.3.
The enemies now keep growing their numbers far longer before they level off. Each attack burst still adds one more bot to every wall, but the ceiling has been raised from four per wall to eight, so a long round can build from sixteen attackers up to as many as thirty-two, and simply outlasting the wave is no longer a safe stalemate.
Walls can no longer be reinforced without limit. A wall now tops out at four times its full strength, so you can still stockpile a buffer but can't build one so high the enemies could never break it; once a wall is maxed, trying to add more keeps the wood in your pack and tells you the wall can't take any more.
An open game can no longer have its round begun while you are the only one in it. Because open games are meant to be shared, the host now needs at least one other player to have joined before starting; to play by yourself, start a single-player game instead.
You can now choose a language chat channel with /channel. Global and local chat are split by channel, so you talk only with others who share yours, while private messages and staff chat still reach everyone regardless; everyone starts on English and your choice is saved for next time. There is also an Unfiltered channel, advised for adult topics, where the word filter never applies.
During a wave you are now told every time the enemies pull back to rest and every time they surge again, each with its own sound and an alert, so you know when it is safe to rebuild and when the next push is starting, not only at the very beginning of the wave.
Enemy bots now attack in a measured, capped pattern instead of piling up without limit: each wave opens with one bot per wall, and every attack burst adds one more to each wall, up to four per wall, sixteen in all, and never beyond that. When a wall falls its bots move onto the walls still standing, but still only up to that four-per-wall cap, so no single wall ever gets swarmed.
Strikes on a wall now swell with danger: the weapon blow and the wall's creak start quiet while a wall is strong and grow louder the more battered it gets, so you can tell by volume which wall is in real trouble.
Gathering wood now reaches five tiles: simply walking near wood scoops up anything within five tiles of you automatically, instead of only the tile you land on, so there is no longer a separate grab key to press.
Reinforcing a wall no longer means leaning right against it: standing within five tiles of a wall now counts from any direction and reinforces whichever wall you are nearest, no matter which way you are facing, and you can hold Shift and Enter to keep placing wood instead of pressing it for every piece.
Larger maps now grow much more wood. Bigger caverns drop several pieces at a time and hold more on the ground at once, so a team spread across a big map is no longer starved for wood the way a small map never was; small maps are unchanged.
New in 2.2.
Cavern walls now start each round between half and full strength, instead of as low as a fifth, so no wall begins so weak that the bots can topple it before you have a chance to reinforce it.
Each player now starts every round carrying a random fifteen to thirty wood, a head start so you can shore up the weakest walls right away instead of waiting for the gathering to pick up.
Voice chat now follows people as they move: a clip plays from wherever the speaker is and tracks them around the map while it plays, so you can hear them walk past you, and you can move around their voice too, just like footsteps.
A wall that has fallen now reads as destroyed instead of zero percent, both when you check a single wall with N, E, S, or W and in the full summary on T.
If you run your own server, new account folders now keep the exact capitalization the player signed up with, for example EllaShey instead of ellashey, so the folders are easier to recognize; signing in still works no matter how you type your name.
Commands that target a player now accept either their username or their current nickname, so you can act on someone by the name you actually see. They also require the target to be online, so you can no longer kick, ban, mute, promote, demote, or warn an offline player, the only exception being unban, which still reaches offline accounts by username since a banned player is always offline. When a name isn't found, the game now tells you whether that player does not exist or is simply not online.
You can now set a nickname with /nickname or /nick, an alternative name shown in place of your username everywhere, for example /nick bob; type /nick default to go back to your account name. Staff can set another player's nickname with /nick followed by their username and the new name, everyone is alerted when a nickname changes, and the /who and staff lists show the real username in parentheses so staff can still tell who is who.
The player events buffer now collects what others are doing across the server: someone creating a room, joining one, deleting one, leaving one, starting an open game you can join, stopping or leaving a game, kicking a player, or going AFK and coming back. Your own actions still confirm to you in the misc buffer instead, so nothing is doubled up.
You can now press D in a game to hear where the wood around you is, listed nearest first as a direction relative to the way you are facing and a rough distance, the same way P locates players. The four-wall strength summary has moved from D to T.
The looping wood sounds now fall silent while a menu is open in a cavern, such as the round menu or the play again prompt, and resume when you close it.
New in 2.1.
Fixed voice chat playing the previous clip instead of the new one, both for hearing your own recording and for other players' messages; each clip now plays fresh as it arrives.
Fixed reviewing a buffer going silent at its edges: comma and period, and shift with them, now always read the current message, so a buffer holding a single message is no longer unresponsive.
New account notices now play and appear in the alerts buffer the moment someone creates an account, before that person even signs in, rather than waiting for their first login.
You can now press P to hear where the other players around you are, listed nearest first, each as a direction relative to the way you are facing and a rough distance, or that no one is nearby. Staff who have raised their flag are named with their rank, and anyone who is away is marked AFK, just like in the other lists.
Reinforcing a wall now adds from five to ten strength per piece of wood, up from one to five, so each piece counts for more against the tougher walls.
Fixed the game dropping its connection when a menu or dialog was left open for a while, most often the results and play again prompt after a round: the network is now kept alive while any menu, dialog, or text box is on screen, so you can take your time without being disconnected.
Fixed the lobby menu opening with tab instead of enter after a connection drop during a game: returning to the lobby now always clears your in-game state, so enter opens the menu as it should.
Cavern walls are now much tougher, with twice the maximum strength they had before, and the enemies swing more slowly, so a round is more about steady upkeep than a frantic scramble. The axe, bat, crowbar, and hammer now hit every one, one and a quarter, one and a half, and two seconds respectively.
You can now go AFK by typing /afk, which starts a ten second countdown of beeps; press any key to cancel it, and once you are away most keys bring you back, while the chat, buffer review, F1 through F5, R, L, G, and escape keys keep working without waking you. Typing /afk again also brings you back. While you are AFK everyone is told in the misc buffer, your name reads as AFK on your chat and in /who and the R, L, and G readouts no matter where you are, and administrators and up can put another player AFK with /afk followed by a name.
The owner account named in the server config is now always a developer and can no longer be demoted, so a self-demote can't strip your powers or revert confusingly on your next sign in.
New in 2.0.
Slash commands that target a player now require the name's capitalization to match exactly, so a wrong-case name like BOB is no longer treated as the account bob; you must type the name the way it really is. Signing in still works regardless of case.
Staff can now raise or lower their staff flag with the new /flag command, off by default and saved to your account: raising it shows your rank to everyone, while lowering it makes you appear as an ordinary player in chat, the R, L, and G readouts, join notices, and the room and game lists. Whenever someone flips their flag, every user hears a sound and gets an alert, for example, Attention! administrator bob has raised their staff flag. Your rank always shows in /who, the staff list, and the connect notices regardless.
If you run your own server, it now refuses to open a second copy of itself, the same way the client already does, so you can't accidentally run two servers at once.
If you run your own server, the owner account named in its config (owner, defaulting to tsatria03) is now always a developer, set when the account is created and re-applied each time it signs in.
Each player's locator beacon now keeps a steady tone of its own instead of its pitch jumping around on every pulse, so you can tell people apart by ear more easily.
Cavern walls are now sturdier, taking about twice as much punishment before they fall, so a round lasts longer and reinforcing goes further.
Enemy waves now come in bursts: the bots attack for a stretch, then pull back to rest for an equal stretch before attacking again, giving you time to rebuild your walls between pushes. Each attack and rest lasts half of that map's build phase, and the bots keep accumulating, so every burst hits harder than the last.
Fixed voice chat only ever sending your first recording; each push-to-talk message you record and send is now its own clip, instead of repeating the first one.
The build phase before each wave is now longer, and wood spawns much faster with more allowed on the ground at once, so you have time to gather wood and strengthen your walls before the enemies arrive.
You can now hear other players picking up and placing wood in 3D, from right where they are doing it, just as dropped wood already sounds.
Administrators and up can now warn a player with /warn, followed by the player's name, a level from 1 to 4, and a reason, for example /warn bob 2 stop spamming. The warned player hears a sound matched to the level and is told who warned them and why; warnings are saved to the player's account, and other staff are quietly notified.
Fixed a major networking bug that stopped players from connecting to a hosted server by its real address; the game now uses plain IPv4, so hosting over a VPN such as Radmin, or over a local network, works as expected. Before this, only connecting to a server on the same computer worked.
Player beacons now start off when you sign in; press F5 to turn them on whenever you want to hear where everyone is.
Joining a game now plays a sound, both for you when you enter and for everyone already in the game when someone new joins. Staff coming online now have their own distinct sound, so you can tell a staff arrival from a regular player's.
Bumping into a cavern wall now plays in 3D, so you can tell by ear which of the four walls you are up against. The menu you open with Tab in a game has been renamed the round menu to set it apart from the game menu on escape, and the build and wave announcements now also collect in the alerts buffer so you can review them.
When a round ends, the game now tells you how long you survived and asks whether to play again or leave. Choose play again to stay in the cavern for another round, or leave to head back to your room; in an open game the next round starts once everyone has answered, and the host begins it.
While a round is running, the host can now pause the whole game for everyone by pressing escape, which freezes the bots, the timers, and the wave until the host resumes from the pause menu. A solo game stays paused for as long as you like, while an open game resumes on its own after a minute and a half, and anyone can still leave during a pause; if the host leaves, another player takes over and the game keeps going.
Once the wave begins, enemies now attack your walls, each armed with an axe, a bat, a crowbar, or a hammer that has its own speed and strength. You hear every strike land at the wall it is hitting, growing louder as that wall weakens, and a wall whose strength runs out breaks open into rubble you can walk across, with its attackers spreading to the walls still standing. The round is lost when all four walls have fallen, at which point you are told how many seconds your team survived before the cavern resets to a fresh set of walls for another round.
In a game you can now gather wood and reinforce your walls. Each of the four cavern walls starts at a random strength; wood drops around the cavern during the round, which you collect by walking over it or pressing Enter to grab everything nearby, and you reinforce a wall by standing at it and pressing Shift plus Enter to spend a piece of wood for a random amount of strength, with no upper limit so you can over-build a wall as a buffer. Press I to hear how much wood you're carrying, N, E, S, or W for a single wall's strength, or D for all four at once; the game menu has also been moved to Tab, leaving Enter free for gathering.
Staff can now mute or ban themselves, just as you can already send yourself a private message. It's on you to use it carefully; a self-mute can be lifted with /unmute, and a host who bans themselves by accident can clear it by editing their account.
New in 1.9.
Staff can now mute a player one channel at a time. Use /mute with a player's name, then a channel, global, local, voice, or all, and optionally a number of seconds; leave the time off for an indefinite mute, and lift it with /unmute and the same channel. A muted player can still use commands but can't chat, use /me, or talk by voice in that channel, and everyone is told in the alerts buffer when a player is muted or unmuted.
