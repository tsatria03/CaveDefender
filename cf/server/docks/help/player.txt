Player help.

This lists the chat commands available to every player. For how to actually play, moving around, chatting, language channels, the players menu, rooms, games, and the rest, read the readme that came with the game. Words like player and message are things you fill in yourself.

Commands.

Press slash to open the chat, type a command, and press enter to send it. Every command here works for everyone, no matter your rank.

/staff: Lists every staff member, online or offline, along with their rank.
/where player: Tells you where an online player is, such as in the lobby, a room, or a game, by their username or their nickname. If that player has personally muted you, their location shows as private.
/report player reason: Reports a player to the staff, for example /report bob kept smashing our own walls on purpose. Everything after the name is your reason, which is required. You can report an online player by their username or nickname, or someone who has already left by their username. Any staff online are alerted right away, and the report is saved so staff can read it later, stamped with where you were when you sent it; the reported player is not told.
/pm player message: Sends a private message to a player, by their username or their nickname. If they are online they receive it at once. If they are offline you can still reach them by their username; the message is then saved and delivered the next time they sign in, and there is no limit on how many you can leave. When you send it, you are told which of the two happened. You cannot send a private message to yourself.
/reply message, or /r message: Sends a private message to the last person who sent you one, so you don't have to type their name. If they have since gone offline, it is saved and delivered when they next sign in, the same as any other offline message.
/me message: Sends an action to everyone, with your name in front, for example /me waves becomes a message reading your name then waves. If you are staff, your rank is shown too.
/nickname name, or /nick name: Sets a nickname, an alternative name shown in place of your username everywhere, for example /nick bob. Type /nick default to go back to your account name. Nicknames are 3 to 24 characters, use the same letters, numbers, hyphens, and underscores as usernames, and must not already be in use; your real username still appears in parentheses in the who and staff lists.
/changemail email: Changes the email on your account, for example /changemail you@example.com. It must contain an at sign and no spaces. Every account needs an email to sign in, but you sign in with your username and password rather than the email itself, and changing it does not sign you out. Your email is private, shown only to staff on the information card.
/changepass: Changes your own password. You are never asked to type it on the chat line; instead a form opens with three masked fields you tab through, your current password, your new password, and the new password again to confirm, and nothing you type is shown. If the current password is wrong, the two new entries do not match, or the new one is not 6 to 24 characters, nothing changes. On success you are returned to the connection menu and signed straight back in, since the game remembers the new password for you.
/delchar: Permanently deletes your own account. It first asks you to confirm on a yes or no warning, then, if you choose yes, to type your password once in a masked box; the wrong password cancels it and nothing is deleted. Deleting cannot be undone: your account and everything on it are gone, everyone is told you deleted your account, and you are returned to the connection menu with that account forgotten on this computer. To delete another player's account instead, an administrator uses /delchar with their name; see the staff help.
/help: Opens the player help, this page, in a readable window. Just type /help on its own; everyone gets this page, staff included. Staff can also open the staff help with /help staff, and /help player reopens this one.
/rules: Opens the server rules in a readable window. Just type /rules on its own for the player rules, or /rules player or /rules staff to choose a page; anyone can read both, including the rules staff are held to, though you must read your own player rules before you may open the staff rules. When you first sign in you must read the rules and then type /rules agree before you can play, or /rules disagree to leave and return to the connection menu. Everyone must read the player rules to agree, and staff must read both the player and staff rules. You only agree once; you are asked again only if the server's rules are updated.
/afk, or /afk reason: Marks you as away. It starts a ten second countdown of beeps; press any key during the countdown to cancel it. Once you are away, everyone is told and your name shows as AFK in the player lists wherever you are, and the next key you press brings you back. Anything you type after /afk is an optional reason, for example /afk making lunch, which is announced once when you go away; leave it off to go away without one.
/channel: Opens a menu to choose your language chat channel; the readme explains what it affects.
/pronoun: Opens a menu to choose the pronouns used to refer to you in messages, such as masculine or feminine. It defaults to singular they, and the none option uses your name in place of a pronoun.
/ignore: Opens a menu of the players online to personally mute, hiding that person's chat and voice from you only; they are not told when you mute them, though if they try to message you they learn they have been muted, and it lasts until you unmute them. Staff cannot be muted. This is the same personal mute as the checkbox on a player's information card.
/ignored: Opens a menu of everyone you are personally muting; choose one to stop muting them and you alone are told they are now unmuted.
/accept: Accepts a pending room or game invitation and takes you in; see the readme for rooms and games.
/reject: Refuses a pending room or game invitation; see the readme for rooms and games.

What only staff can do.

A few things are reserved for staff, the lowest staff rank being moderator. As a regular player you cannot use staff chat, skip a game's build phase, stop a game you don't host, or delete a room that isn't yours or that still has people in it. The full staff list is in the staff help.

For server hosts.

The word filter list lives in data/preffs/chatfilter.svr, one rule per line, read fresh each message so it can be edited while the server runs. A rule is either re= followed by a regular expression matched anywhere in the message, or a plain word or phrase matched as a substring. Messages are lowercased before matching, so write rules in lowercase, and lines beginning with a number sign are comments. The crash string protection is hardcoded separately and is not in this file.
