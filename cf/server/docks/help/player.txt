Player help.

This lists the chat commands available to every player. For how to actually play, moving around, chatting, language channels, the players menu, rooms, games, and the rest, read the readme that came with the game. Words like player and message are things you fill in yourself.

Commands.

Press slash to open the chat, type a command, and press enter to send it. Every command here works for everyone, no matter your rank.

/staff: Lists every staff member, online or offline, along with their rank.
/leaderboard mode, or /lb mode: Opens the server-wide leaderboard for a game mode, ranking every account by its best round in that mode from highest to lowest, whether or not that player is online. Type /lb on its own to pick PVE or EVP from a menu, or name the mode directly in capitals, such as /lb PVE or /lb EVP. Only players who have cleared at least one round in that mode appear, and your own row is marked with your name. Choose a row to open that player's information card if they are online, or you are told they are offline.
/where player: Tells you where an online player is, such as in the lobby, a room, or a game, by their username or their nickname. If that player has personally muted you, their location shows as private.
/report player reason: Reports a player to the staff, for example /report bob kept smashing our own walls on purpose. Everything after the name is your reason, which is required. You can report an online player by their username or nickname, or someone who has already left by their username. Any staff online are alerted right away, and the report is saved so staff can read it later, stamped with where you were when you sent it; the reported player is not told.
/feedback message, or /fb message: Sends feedback or a bug report to the developers, for example /feedback the crossbow makes no sound sometimes. There is no player to name; everything after the command is your message, which is required. Unlike a report it names no one and is not about another player, it is about the game or the server itself. It is saved for the developers to read later, stamped with your name and where you were when you sent it, and is not announced to anyone.
/pm player message: Sends a private message to a player, by their username or their nickname. If they are online they receive it at once. If they are offline you can still reach them by their username; the message is then saved and delivered the next time they sign in, and there is no limit on how many you can leave. When you send it, you are told which of the two happened. You cannot send a private message to yourself.
/reply message, or /r message: Sends a private message to the last person who sent you one, so you don't have to type their name. If they have since gone offline, it is saved and delivered when they next sign in, the same as any other offline message.
/me message: Sends an action to everyone, with your name in front, for example /me waves becomes a message reading your name then waves. If you are staff, your rank is shown too.
/nickname name, or /nick name: Sets a nickname, an alternative name shown in place of your username everywhere, for example /nick bobert. Type /nick default to go back to your account name. Nicknames are 3 to 24 characters, use the same letters, numbers, hyphens, and underscores as usernames, and must not already be in use; your real username still appears in parentheses in the who and staff lists.
/changemail email: Changes the email on your account, for example /changemail you@example.com. It must contain an at sign and no spaces. Every account needs an email to sign in, but you sign in with your username and password rather than the email itself, and changing it does not sign you out. Your email is private, shown only to staff on the information card.
/changepass: Changes your own password. You are never asked to type it on the chat line; instead a form opens with three masked fields you tab through, your current password, your new password, and the new password again to confirm, and nothing you type is shown. If the current password is wrong, the two new entries do not match, or the new one is not 6 to 24 characters, nothing changes. On success you are returned to the connection menu and signed straight back in, since the game remembers the new password for you.
/delchar: Permanently deletes your own account. It first asks you to confirm on a yes or no warning, then, if you choose yes, to type your password once in a masked box; the wrong password cancels it and nothing is deleted. Deleting cannot be undone: your account and everything on it are gone, everyone is told you deleted your account, and you are returned to the connection menu with that account forgotten on this computer. To delete another player's account instead, an administrator uses /delchar with their name; see the staff help.
/help: Opens the player help, this page, in a readable window. Just type /help on its own; everyone gets this page, staff included. Staff can also open the staff help with /help staff, and /help player reopens this one.
/rules: Opens the server rules in a readable window. Just type /rules on its own for the player rules, or /rules player or /rules staff to choose a page; anyone can read both, including the rules staff are held to, though you must read your own player rules before you may open the staff rules. When you first sign in you must read the rules, then agree from the menu that appears when you close the page before you can play, or decline there to leave and return to the connection menu. Everyone must read the player rules to agree, and staff must read both the player and staff rules. You only agree once; you are asked again only if the server's rules are updated.
/afk, or /afk reason: Marks you as away. It starts a ten second countdown of beeps; press any key during the countdown to cancel it. Once you are away, everyone is told and your name shows as AFK in the player lists wherever you are, and the next key you press brings you back. Anything you type after /afk is an optional reason, for example /afk making lunch, which is announced once when you go away; leave it off to go away without one. The server can also put you away on its own after a stretch with no keys pressed, going straight to away with no countdown or reason; any key still brings you back the same way, and how long that takes, or whether it happens at all, is set by the server host.
/channel: Opens a menu to choose your language chat channel; the readme explains what it affects.
/pronoun: Opens a menu to choose the pronouns used to refer to you in messages, such as masculine or feminine. It defaults to singular they, and the none option uses your name in place of a pronoun.
/ignore: Opens a menu of the players online to personally mute, hiding that person's chat and voice from you only; they are not told when you mute them, though if they try to message you they learn they have been muted, and it lasts until you unmute them. Staff cannot be muted. This is the same personal mute as the checkbox on a player's information card.
/ignored: Opens a menu of everyone you are personally muting; choose one to stop muting them and you alone are told they are now unmuted.
Invitations to a room or game are answered right where you are, in the lobby or a room: press Alt plus A to accept, or Alt plus D to deny. An invitation lasts two minutes before it expires.
/invtime: Tells you how many seconds you have left to answer your pending room or game invitation. See the readme for rooms and games.

What only staff can do.

A few things are reserved for staff, the lowest staff rank being moderator. As a regular player you cannot use staff chat, skip a game's build phase, stop a game you don't host, or delete a room that isn't yours or that still has people in it. The full staff list is in the staff help.

For server hosts.

Everything the server reads and writes lives in the data folder beside it: player accounts under data/players, one folder per account, the server's settings under data/preffs, and the weapon and item values under data/equipment. In the hand-edited files described below, blank lines are skipped and lines beginning with a number sign are comments.

The server-wide settings live in data/preffs/main, read when the server starts. conf.svr holds the port, the userlimit player cap, the owners list, a comma-separated list of up to three account names all treated as the one owner and always forced to developer, the rules version rulesver, which you bump to make everyone read and agree to the rules again and which never goes below 1, ruleswitch, which you set to 0 only if you deliberately want the rules agreement gate off, and the global chat anti-spam guard: spamguard turns it on or off, and a player who sends spamcount messages within spamtime milliseconds is muted in global for spamdir seconds.

Note that the server rewrites conf.svr itself when the owner list changes, so comments in that one file do not survive. motd.svr is the message of the day, also changeable live with /motd. peak.svr and reports.svr are the server's own records of the player-count peak and filed reports; leave them alone.

The word filter list lives in data/preffs/main/chatfilter.svr, one rule per line, read fresh each message so it can be edited while the server runs. A rule is either re= followed by a regular expression matched anywhere in the message, or a plain word or phrase matched as a substring. Messages are lowercased before matching, so write rules in lowercase. The crash string protection is hardcoded separately and is not in this file.

The language channels and pronoun options are lists in data/preffs/main, one option per line, re-read whenever the file changes so edits apply without a restart. languages.svr lines are the channel name followed by its native name in parentheses, such as French (Francais). pronouns.svr lines are the option's name followed by its five grammatical forms in parentheses, separated by slashes, in the order subject, object, possessive, possessive with s, and reflexive, such as Masculine/male (he/him/his/his/himself).

The weapon values live in data/equipment/weapons, one file per weapon inside a folder per class: melee weapons are .mel files, archery .arc, explosive .exp, and artillery .art. Any value written as two numbers with a comma, such as damage=6,12, is a range rolled fresh on each use, and a single number is fixed. The fields are damage, melee true or false, swing speed and spam time for a melee weapon's windup and recovery in milliseconds, fire mode 0 for one shot per press or 1 for full auto paced by repeat time, range in tiles, and for ranged weapons bullet speed in milliseconds per tile of flight, ammo type, and max ammo for the magazine size. The firebomb's status line sets its wall fire as type, duration, tick, and per-tick damage. These load when the server starts, so edits take a restart, and the set of weapons is fixed by the game: editing a file tunes that weapon, but adding a new file does not create a new one.

The item values live in data/equipment/items: each wood kind is a .mtr file with reinforce, the wall strength one placed piece adds, starter, the amount given at round start, and bonus, the best-round reward, while each ammo kind is a .amt file with starter and bonus. All accept a fixed number or a min,max range and load at server start.

The game tuning lives in data/preffs/game, read when the server starts. In conf.gvr, lines prefixed item set the defaults for every item kind: count lines pair a map size with how many pieces drop per spawn tick, such as itemcount_big=75:3, cap lines pair a size with how many may lie on the ground at once, and itemspawn is the min,max milliseconds between drops. The size words in the key names are labels only; the number in the value decides which map the line applies to. Prefixing a line wood or ammo instead overrides just that item kind, so ammo can be paced separately from wood. wallbase is the walls' full strength, 400 unless changed, and the walls' random starting strengths, the reinforce limit choices, and the builder bots' fortify cap all scale with it automatically.

In effects.gvr, reverb sets what the cave interior's reverberation sounds like and muffle sets the through-the-wall filter, each as an effect name and its numbers separated by colons; the game itself decides when each effect applies, so these only change the flavor, everywhere they are heard, voice chat included.
