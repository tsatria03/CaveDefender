Staff help.

This covers everything staff can do that regular players cannot, both through commands and otherwise, and the lowest rank each thing needs. The staff ranks, from lowest to highest, are moderator, administrator, and developer, and a higher rank can always do everything a lower rank can. Words like player, channel, rank, message, and seconds are things you fill in yourself.

Beyond commands. Moderator and up.

These are not commands; they are things staff do through the game's chat keys and menus.

Staff chat: Press apostrophe to talk on the staff channel, which only staff can send to or hear. Staff messages collect in their own buffer that regular players never see.
Skip the build phase: In a game you host, the game menu lets you skip the build phase and start the wave early. A host who is not staff cannot skip; their build timer always runs its full length.

Stop a game from inside it: The menu you open with escape in a game lets you stop a game you are in even when you don't host it. A regular player can only stop a game from that menu if they host it.
Stop any game: A room's Stop game list shows you every game in that room so you can stop any of them. A regular player only sees games they host in that list.

Delete any room: You can delete any room, even one with other players inside or games still running. A regular host can only delete their own room, and only when alone in it with no games.

Commands. Moderator and up.

Where a command takes a player, you can give either their username or their current nickname, and the player must be online. The one exception is unban, which by nature acts on a banned, and therefore offline, account, so it takes the username and works while the player is offline. If a name can't be matched, the game tells you whether that player does not exist or is simply not online.

/mute player channel, or /mute player channel seconds: Silences a player in one channel: global, local, voice, or all for those channels at once. Leave the time off, or use 0, for an indefinite mute. You can mute anyone at or below your own rank, never above, so a staff member's public channels can be muted too; staff chat itself can never be muted. The muted player can still use commands, but not chat, /me, or voice in that channel; everyone is told in the alerts buffer. The mute is remembered even if the player leaves and comes back. You can mute yourself too; lift it with /unmute on the same channel.
/unmute player channel: Lifts a mute early in that channel, or use all for those channels.
/flag: Toggles whether your staff rank is shown to regular players. It starts off, so by default you look like an ordinary player in chat, the who's-around readouts on R, L, and G, join notices, and the room and game lists; turn it on when you want players to see your rank and know to listen, and off again to blend in. Flipping it announces to every user, with a sound and an alert, that you raised or lowered your staff flag, for example, Attention! administrator bob has raised their staff flag. Your rank always shows in /who, the staff list, and the online and offline notices regardless. The setting is saved to your account.
/nickname player altname, or /nick player altname: Sets another online player's nickname, the same way anyone sets their own with /nick. The new name is 3 to 24 characters with the same allowed characters as usernames and must not already be in use, everyone is alerted to the change, and the player's real username keeps showing in parentheses in the who and staff lists. All staff can use this.
/promote player rank: Raises a player to a higher rank, for example /promote bob moderator. Two limits always apply: the rank you set must be at or below your own, and the player's current rank must also be at or below your own. In practice: a moderator can promote a player up to moderator; an administrator can promote up to administrator, but not to developer; a developer can promote anyone to any rank. The new rank must be higher than the player's current one.
/demote player rank: Lowers a player to a lower rank, for example /demote bob player. The same two limits apply, so: a moderator can demote a moderator down to player; an administrator can demote administrators or moderators, but can't touch a developer; a developer can demote anyone. The new rank must be lower than the player's current one. You can also promote or demote yourself within these limits, which means you can step yourself down a rank but never raise yourself. The one exception is the owner account named in the server config, which is always a developer; you can't demote yourself as a developer, so the owner can never lose the rank.
/notify message: Sends a notification, with a sound, to everyone on the server.
/notifyplayer player message, or /notplayer player message: Sends a notification, with a sound, to a single player.

Administrator and up.

/afk player: Toggles another player's away state at once, with no countdown: an active player is put away, and a player who is already away is brought back. They hear the matching sound, everyone is told, and their name shows as AFK in the lists while they are away. Anyone can also mark themselves away by typing /afk with no name; see the player help.
/kick player: Removes a player from the server. They can sign back in right away. A kick can target anyone, including other staff, and even yourself, which simply drops you to the connection menu so you can sign back in.
/ban player, or /ban player seconds: Bans a player so they can't sign in. Leave the time off for a permanent ban. You can't ban someone above your own rank, but you can ban yourself, so use it carefully: you'll be banned like anyone else. If you do it by accident as the host, clear it by editing your account.
/unban player: Lifts a ban.
/warn player level reason: Warns a player, for example /warn bob 2 stop spamming. The level is 1 to 4, where 4 is the most severe, and sets the warning sound the player hears along with your reason. You can warn anyone at or below your own rank. The player sees who warned them and why; you are told their running total, each warning is saved to their account, and other staff get a quiet note about it.
/motd message: Changes the server's message of the day.
/restart seconds, or /rt seconds: Warns everyone with a countdown, then shuts the server down. The time can be from 30 to 3600 seconds.
/fastrestart, or /ft: Shuts the server down right away, with no warning.
