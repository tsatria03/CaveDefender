New in 2.0.
When a round ends, the game now tells you how long you survived and asks whether to play again or leave. Choose play again to stay in the cavern for another round, or leave to head back to your room; in an open game the next round starts once everyone has answered, and the host begins it.
While a round is running, the host can now pause the whole game for everyone by pressing escape, which freezes the bots, the timers, and the wave until the host resumes from the pause menu. A solo game stays paused for as long as you like, while an open game resumes on its own after a minute and a half, and anyone can still leave during a pause; if the host leaves, another player takes over and the game keeps going.
Once the wave begins, enemies now attack your walls, each armed with an axe, a bat, a crowbar, or a hammer that has its own speed and strength. You hear every strike land at the wall it is hitting, growing louder as that wall weakens, and a wall whose strength runs out breaks open into rubble you can walk across, with its attackers spreading to the walls still standing. The round is lost when all four walls have fallen, at which point you are told how many seconds your team survived before the cavern resets to a fresh set of walls for another round.
In a game you can now gather wood and reinforce your walls. Each of the four cavern walls starts at a random strength; wood drops around the cavern during the round, which you collect by walking over it or pressing Enter to grab everything nearby, and you reinforce a wall by standing at it and pressing Shift plus Enter to spend a piece of wood for a random amount of strength, with no upper limit so you can over-build a wall as a buffer. Press I to hear how much wood you're carrying, N, E, S, or W for a single wall's strength, or D for all four at once; the game menu has also been moved to Tab, leaving Enter free for gathering.
Staff can now mute or ban themselves, just as you can already send yourself a private message. It's on you to use it carefully; a self-mute can be lifted with /unmute, and a host who bans themselves by accident can clear it by editing their account.
New in 1.9.
Staff can now mute a player one channel at a time. Use /mute with a player's name, then a channel, global, local, voice, or all, and optionally a number of seconds; leave the time off for an indefinite mute, and lift it with /unmute and the same channel. A muted player can still use commands but can't chat, use /me, or talk by voice in that channel, and everyone is told in the alerts buffer when a player is muted or unmuted. You can mute anyone at or below your own rank, including a staff member's public channels, but staff chat itself can never be muted.
Leaving the game now returns you to the connection menu instead of the main menu, so you can sign back in right away; from there, back still takes you to the main menu.
Creating an account no longer signs you in straight away. Once it's made, the game asks whether you want to log in now; choose no and you're returned to the connection menu, where your new account is already saved for signing in.
Fixed timed mutes and bans being off by up to a couple of minutes, so a mute or ban now lasts exactly as long as it was set for, and the remaining time is reported accurately.
The lobby, the rooms, and the game caverns now play their own background music over the ambience.
Each space now has its own background ambience that plays across the whole map, so the lobby, the rooms, and the game caverns each have their own atmosphere as you move around them.
The lobby, room, and in-game menus are now handled by the server. If the server is briefly down or busy when you open one, the menu now waits for it and then appears, the same way chat does, instead of failing to open or coming up empty.
If the connection drops while you are signed in, the game now keeps running and spends up to thirty seconds quietly trying to reconnect and sign you back in, rather than reporting the server offline right away; if it gets back on you return to the lobby, and you can press escape at any time to stop waiting.
The ping timeout is now fifteen seconds, and the ping sound now plays when a ping is reported lost, just as it does when one comes back.
You can now press escape while signing in to cancel the connection attempt and return to the connection menu, instead of waiting for it to finish or time out.
New in 1.8.
The Join game menu now lists games that are already underway as well, but tells you a game has already started, so you can't join it, if you pick one.
Fixed the Join game menu failing to open, and reading out a line of raw text instead, whenever a room already had a game in it.
Fixed leaving a game you hadn't started yet closing it for good, so you had no way back in. A game that has not begun its round now stays open in your room after everyone steps out, and you can rejoin it from Join game, including your own single-player game; it closes only when stopped or when the room closes.
Pressing R, L, or G now gives you a spoken glance at who is around and where, much like /who does for the whole server. In the lobby, R lists the open rooms, L the players in the lobby, and G every game on the server; inside a room, R is your room and G is your room's games; and in a game, G lists your game. All of these readouts, and /who, now use correct grammar for empty spaces and for a single player or game.
Fixed the room list showing a phantom player in a room nobody had joined, and the number of games in progress now shows the real count for each room instead of always reading zero.
The help pages you open with /help have been rewritten to clearly lay out what each rank can and can't do, covering not just commands but rooms, games, and the chat channels, so the limits for players and staff are all in one place. This is handy for server hosters too.
Staff can now stop a game from inside it, not just from the room menu, so a moderator or above who is in a game can end it without leaving first.
Leaving or stopping a game now asks you to confirm first, so you don't end a round or drop out by accident.
You can now join and stop games from your room menu. Join a game lists the open games that still have a free slot so you can jump in, and stop a game lets you end a game you host, while staff can stop any game.
You can now start a game from inside your room, choosing a game mode and a map size, and you'll be taken into its cavern. While in a game, press escape for a pause menu where you can leave or stop it.
New in 1.7.
You can now hear other players moving around you. Their footsteps, and the sound of them bumping into walls, play from their spot on the map on whatever surface they are walking on, so you can tell where people are as they move.
Each room is now its own space. When you are in a room you stand on the room's own map and only hear, locate, and voice chat with the other players in that room, kept separate from the lobby and from other rooms; leaving a room puts you back in the lobby with everyone there.
Updated the todo list file with the latest bug fix and feature improvements made to the game.
You can now use local chat with the backslash key to talk to everyone in your current space, the lobby or your room, separate from global chat. As with the other chats, /me and slash commands work from it too.
Fixed being unable to reconnect with an "already logged in" error after a connection dropped or the game was closed. Signing in now takes over your old session instead of being blocked by it.
Staff can now talk privately with staff chat: press the apostrophe key to send a message only other staff see. You can run slash commands and /me from it too, just like normal chat, and staff messages collect in their own staff chats buffer.
The chat buffers have been renamed for clarity: chats is now global chats, and pm's is now private chats, making room for more chat types to come.
Creating a room now plays a sound.
Your public and private rooms are now saved to your account and come back automatically the next time you sign in, with your private room's password remembered.
If you run your own server, each player account is now stored as a folder of small files, one per setting, instead of a single combined file, so you can read or change an individual setting by hand.
New in 1.6.
If you host a private room, you can now use Set password to change its password and Retrieve password to hear or copy the current one.
The host can now Kick a player back to the lobby from a room, and Delete room now works with a confirmation. Staff can delete any room, while a non-staff host can only delete their own when no one else is inside; anyone in a deleted room is returned to the lobby.
You can now have up to two of your own rooms at once: one public and one private.
You can now join a private room: choose Join a private room, pick one from the list, and enter its password to get in.
You can now join a public room. Choose Join a public room from the lobby menu to see the open rooms, who hosts each one, how many players are in it, and how many games are in progress, then pick one to join.
New accounts now require a username and a password that are each between 6 and 24 characters.
When creating an account or signing in, if a field is left empty or filled in incorrectly, the cursor now returns to the field you need to fix instead of leaving you to find it.
You can now create a private room as well, protected by a password between 6 and 24 characters that you set when you make it; others will need that password to join.
You can now press Enter in the lobby to open a menu and create a public room, then press Enter inside the room to open its menu and leave.
Staff are now called out when they connect. When a staff member comes online, goes offline, is kicked, or is banned, their rank is shown in front of their name, so everyone can see who is staff coming and going.
New in 1.5.
Staff commands you don't have the rank to use are now reported as unknown commands, the same as a command that doesn't exist, so the commands meant for staff stay hidden from regular players.
You can now type /me followed by an action to send it to everyone with your name, and your rank if you are staff, in front of it, for example /me waves.
You can now reply to the last private message you received with /reply, or /r, followed by your message, without having to type the sender's name.
You can now open a help page listing the commands you can use, what each one does, and which rank it needs. Regular players just type /help to read the player help, while staff can choose a page with /help player or /help staff.
Administrators and developers can now ban a player with /ban, followed by their name and, optionally, a number of seconds; leave the time off for a permanent ban, and lift it early with /unban. A banned player is removed from the server and can't sign back in until the ban ends. Kicking and banning are now limited to administrators and developers.
Staff can now silence a player with /mute, followed by their name and, optionally, a number of seconds; leave the time off for an indefinite mute, and lift it early with /unmute. A muted player can't use chat or voice, hears a sound when muted and unmuted, and the mute is remembered even if they leave and come back.
Moderators can now use /promote and /demote as well, but only to move players to and from the moderator rank; changing anyone to or from a higher rank is still limited to administrators and developers.
Staff who use /promote and /demote now choose the exact rank to set, for example /demote name administrator, so a player can be moved up or down to a specific rank rather than always dropping straight to player.
When someone creates a new account and joins for the first time, everyone now hears a sound and is told that the player just created a new account, instead of the usual coming-online message.
Staff can now send notifications that play an attention sound. Use /notify followed by a message to alert everyone on the server, or /notifyplayer, also /notplayer, followed by a player's name and a message to alert just that one player.
New in 1.4.
Administrators and developers can now change a player's rank with the /promote and /demote commands, and there is a new moderator rank that sits below administrator. Anyone whose rank changes is told what they were promoted or demoted from and to.
Staff can now remove a player with the /kick command, followed by that player's name. The kicked player is told they've been kicked and returned to the connection menu, and everyone else is told who was kicked.
If the server ever goes offline, whether from one of those commands or an unexpected drop, the game now notices, tells you the server has gone offline, and returns you to the connection menu so you can sign back in once it is up again.
Staff can now shut the server down with two new commands. Use /restart, or /rt, followed by a number of seconds from 30 to 3600 to warn everyone with a countdown that announces the minutes or seconds remaining, or use /fastrestart, or /ft, to shut down right away with no warning.
Added the /staff command, which lists every admin and developer on the game, online or offline, so you can always see who is staff.

Server staff now stand out. When an admin or developer signs in they're told their rank, and their rank now appears in front of their name in public chat and private messages, so everyone can see who is staff.
If you run your own server, player accounts now store a rank of player, admin, or developer, saved in each account's file. Set someone to admin or developer to give them staff powers, and that rank now sticks between sessions instead of resetting each time they sign in.
The server now checks that your game is up to date when you sign in. If your version does not match the server's, you'll be told to update rather than running into problems later on.
Voice chat now reaches other players. When you send a voice message it plays for everyone else from your position on the map, so you can hear who is talking and where they are. Whether you also hear your own voice is controlled by the option in the game settings.
